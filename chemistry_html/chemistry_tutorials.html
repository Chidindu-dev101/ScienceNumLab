Importance of Units in Calculations

Units are a fundamental aspect of calculations, though often overlooked as merely a way to earn marks. However, they play a crucial role in solving problems, especially in objective questions, in ways that might not be immediately apparent.

In chemistry, formulas also help derive units. For example, the formula for molar concentration is \(\frac{\text{Amount of Substance in moles}}{\text{Volume in } \text{dm}^3}\) . The unit of volume is \( \text{dm}^3 \) and the unit for Amount of Substance in moles is \( \text{mol} \) . This tells us that the unit of molar concentration is \( \text{mol} \text{dm}^{-3} \) .
