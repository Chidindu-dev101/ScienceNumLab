Math Trigometry Tricks

Important Basic Trig Trick

A helpful mnemonic to remember trigonometric ratios is SOH-CAH-TOA:

Sine (\(\sin \theta\)): The sine of an angle is the ratio of the Opposite side to the Hypotenuse. \( \sin \theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} \)

Cosine (\(\cos \theta\)): The cosine of an angle is the ratio of the Adjacent side to the Hypotenuse. \( \cos \theta = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} \)

Tangent (\(\tan \theta\)): The tangent of an angle is the ratio of the Opposite side to the Adjacent side. \( \tan \theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}} \)

Using SOH-CAH-TOA makes it easier to recall these fundamental trigonometric relationships and solve problems involving right triangles.

Sine Function

The formula for sine is \( \sin \theta = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}} \). The sine function measures the ratio of the length of the opposite side to the hypotenuse in a right triangle.
\( \sin \theta = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}} \)

Cosine Function

The cosine function is defined as \( \cos \theta \). It represents the ratio of the adjacent side to the hypotenuse in a right triangle.
\( \cos \theta \)

Tangent Function

The tangent function is defined as \( \tan \theta \). It represents the ratio of the opposite side to the adjacent side in a right triangle.
\( \tan \theta \)

Cotangent Function

The cotangent function is defined as \( \cot \theta \). It represents the reciprocal of the tangent function, or the ratio of the adjacent side to the opposite side in a right triangle.
\( \cot \theta \)

Secant Function

The secant function is defined as \( \sec \theta \). It represents the reciprocal of the cosine function, or the ratio of the hypotenuse to the adjacent side in a right triangle.
\( \sec \theta \)

Cosecant Function

The formula for cosecant is \( \csc \theta = \frac{\text{hypotenuse}}{\text{opposite}} \). The cosecant function is the reciprocal of the sine function.
\( \csc \theta = \frac{\text{hypotenuse}}{\text{opposite}} \)

Pythagorean Identity

The Pythagorean identity is \( \sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1 \). This identity is derived from the Pythagorean theorem.
\( \sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1 \)

Sum and Difference Formulas

The formulas for the sum and difference of angles are \( \sin(A \pm B) = \sin A \cos B \pm \cos A \sin B \) and \( \cos(A \pm B) = \cos A \cos B \mp \sin A \sin B \).
\( \sin(A \pm B) = \sin A \cos B \pm \cos A \sin B \)
\( \cos(A \pm B) = \cos A \cos B \mp \sin A \sin B \)

Double Angle Formulas

The formulas for double angles are \( \sin 2\theta = 2 \sin \theta \cos \theta \) and \( \cos 2\theta = \cos^2 \theta - \sin^2 \theta \).
\( \sin 2\theta = 2 \sin \theta \cos \theta \)
\( \cos 2\theta = \cos^2 \theta - \sin^2 \theta \)

Half-Angle Formulas

The formulas for half angles are \( \sin \frac{\theta}{2} = \pm \sqrt{\frac{1 - \cos \theta}{2}} \) and \( \cos \frac{\theta}{2} = \pm \sqrt{\frac{1 + \cos \theta}{2}} \).
\( \sin \frac{\theta}{2} = \pm \sqrt{\frac{1 - \cos \theta}{2}} \)
\( \cos \frac{\theta}{2} = \pm \sqrt{\frac{1 + \cos \theta}{2}} \)

Product-to-Sum Formulas

The formulas for converting products to sums are \( \sin A \sin B = \frac{1}{2} [\cos(A - B) - \cos(A + B)] \) and \( \cos A \cos B = \frac{1}{2} [\cos(A - B) + \cos(A + B)] \).
\( \sin A \sin B = \frac{1}{2} [\cos(A - B) - \cos(A + B)] \)
\( \cos A \cos B = \frac{1}{2} [\cos(A - B) + \cos(A + B)] \)

Sum-to-Product Formulas

The formulas for converting sums to products are \( \sin A + \sin B = 2 \sin \left( \frac{A + B}{2} \right) \cos \left( \frac{A - B}{2} \right) \) and \( \cos A + \cos B = 2 \cos \left( \frac{A + B}{2} \right) \cos \left( \frac{A - B}{2} \right) \).
\( \sin A + \sin B = 2 \sin \left( \frac{A + B}{2} \right) \cos \left( \frac{A - B}{2} \right) \)
\( \cos A + \cos B = 2 \cos \left( \frac{A + B}{2} \right) \cos \left( \frac{A - B}{2} \right) \)

Law of Sines

The formula for the law of sines is \( \frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C} \). This law relates the lengths of the sides of a triangle to the sines of its angles.
\( \frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C} \)

Law of Cosines

The formula for the law of cosines is \( c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab \cos C \). This law relates the lengths of the sides of a triangle to the cosine of one of its angles.
\( c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab \cos C \)

Law of Tangents

The formula for the law of tangents is \( \frac{a - b}{a + b} = \frac{\tan \left( \frac{A - B}{2} \right)}{\tan \left( \frac{A + B}{2} \right)} \). This law relates the lengths of the sides of a triangle to the tangents of half the sum and half the difference of the angles.
\( \frac{a - b}{a + b} = \frac{\tan \left( \frac{A - B}{2} \right)}{\tan \left( \frac{A + B}{2} \right)} \)

Inverse Trigonometric Functions

The formula for the inverse sine function is \( \sin^{-1} x \). The inverse sine function, also known as arcsine, returns the angle whose sine is \( x \).
\( \sin^{-1} x \)

Trigonometric Equations

The solutions for trigonometric equations are given by \( \sin \theta = k \) and \( \cos \theta = k \).
\( \sin \theta = k \)
\( \cos \theta = k \)

Trigonometric Identities

Important trigonometric identities include \( 1 + \tan^2 \theta = \sec^2 \theta \), \( 1 + \cot^2 \theta = \csc^2 \theta \), and \( \sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1 \).
\( 1 + \tan^2 \theta = \sec^2 \theta \)
\( 1 + \cot^2 \theta = \csc^2 \theta \)
\( \sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1 \)
