Importance of Units in Calculations

Units are a fundamental aspect of calculations, though often overlooked as merely a way to earn marks. However, they play a crucial role in solving problems, especially in objective questions, in ways that might not be immediately apparent.

Consider the unit of speed, which is meters per second \( \text{ms}^{-1} \). From this unit, we can infer that speed is the distance covered divided by the time taken because \( \text{m} = \text{meters} \) and \( \text{s} = \text{seconds} \). Formulas can help derive units.
For example, in physics, the formula for stress is \( \frac{\text{Force}}{\text{Cross-sectional area}} \)
This tells us that the unit of stress is \( \text{Nm}^{-2} \) since force is \(\text{N (Newtons)}\) and area is \( \text{m}^2 \) and it is \( \frac{\text{Newtons}}{\text{meters}^2} \)

Speed and Velocity \( \text{ms}^{-1} \)

The formula for speed is \(\frac{distance}{time}\) while velocity is \(\frac{displacement}{time}\) since displacement and distance are in meters m and time is in seconds s.THe unit of velocity and speed is \( \text{ms}^{-1} \) .
\(v\,=\,\frac{s}{t}\)

where \(s\,=\,\text{distance}\) and \(t\,=\,\text{time}\)
